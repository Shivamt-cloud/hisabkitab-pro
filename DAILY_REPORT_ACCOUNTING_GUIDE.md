# Daily Report - Comprehensive Accounting Guide

## Overview

The Daily Report now provides a complete accounting statement showing all financial transactions, cash flow, and profit/loss calculations for the day.

## Sections in Daily Report

### 1. **Opening Balance (Morning)**
- Shows the opening cash balance entered in the morning
- Displays cash denomination breakdown (notes and coins)
- Total opening amount

### 2. **Today's Sales Collections**
- **Cash Sales**: Amount collected in cash
- **UPI Sales**: Amount collected via UPI
- **Card Sales**: Amount collected via card
- **Other Sales**: Amount collected via other payment methods
- **Total Collections**: Sum of all payment methods

### 3. **Today's Expenses**
- **Cash Expenses**: Expenses paid in cash
- **UPI Expenses**: Expenses paid via UPI
- **Card Expenses**: Expenses paid via card
- **Other Expenses**: Expenses paid via other methods
- **Total Expenses**: Sum of all expenses

### 4. **Detailed Expenses by Sales Person**
- Shows all expenses grouped by sales person
- Each sales person section shows:
  - Individual expense items
  - Date, type, description, payment method
  - Amount for each expense
  - Total for that sales person

### 5. **All Expenses (Detailed List)**
- Complete table of all expenses
- Columns: Date, Type, Description, Sales Person, Payment Method, Amount
- Sorted by date (newest first)

### 6. **Profit & Loss Calculation**
Shows step-by-step calculation:

1. **Total Sales**: All sales for the day
2. **Total Costing**: Cost of items actually sold (item-wise)
3. **Gross Profit (Items)**: Sales - Costing
4. **Total Expenses**: All expenses (excluding opening/closing)
5. **Net Profit / Loss**: Gross Profit - Expenses
6. **Profit Margins**: Shows percentage margins

### 7. **Cash Reconciliation**
Complete cash flow statement:

1. **Opening Cash Balance**: Starting cash
2. **Cash Sales Received**: Cash collected from sales
3. **Cash Expenses Paid**: Cash spent
4. **Expected Closing Cash**: Opening + Cash Sales - Cash Expenses
5. **Actual Closing Cash**: Cash counted at end of day (if entered)
6. **Difference**: Actual - Expected (shows if cash matches)
7. **Closing Cash Breakdown**: Denomination breakdown of closing cash

## How to Read the Report

### Example Flow:

**Morning:**
- Opening Balance: ₹5,000 (10×₹500 notes)

**During Day:**
- Sales: ₹10,000
  - Cash: ₹6,000
  - UPI: ₹3,000
  - Card: ₹1,000
- Expenses: ₹2,000
  - Cash: ₹1,500
  - UPI: ₹500

**Evening:**
- Expected Closing: ₹9,500 (5,000 + 6,000 - 1,500)
- Actual Closing: ₹9,500 (entered by sales person)
- Difference: ₹0 ✓

**Profit Calculation:**
- Total Sales: ₹10,000
- Total Costing: ₹6,000 (cost of items sold)
- Gross Profit: ₹4,000
- Total Expenses: ₹2,000
- Net Profit: ₹2,000

## Key Features

1. **Item-wise Profit**: Only calculates profit on items actually sold, not entire purchases
2. **Payment Method Breakdown**: Shows exactly how money was collected and spent
3. **Cash Reconciliation**: Verifies cash matches expected amount
4. **Detailed Expenses**: Every expense is listed individually
5. **Sales Person Tracking**: See expenses by each sales person
6. **Complete Accounting**: All transactions are accounted for

## Benefits

- **Clear Accounting**: No confusion about money flow
- **Cash Verification**: Automatically checks if cash matches
- **Profit Tracking**: Accurate profit/loss calculation
- **Expense Audit**: Every expense is visible and traceable
- **Payment Method Analysis**: See which payment methods are used most

## Notes

- Opening/Closing balances are excluded from total expenses
- Profit is calculated item-wise (sale price - purchase price per item)
- Cash reconciliation only works if opening balance is entered
- Difference shows in green if matches, red if there's a mismatch
- All amounts are in Indian Rupees (₹)
